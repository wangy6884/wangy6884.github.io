Welcome to Math Series

In Math, We have three types of series, one is called the Power Series, one is called the Fourier Series and the last one is called the Phase Series. All are used to represent a function. Each one has its view.

Combining the Power Series with the Euler's eqution, we can produce the Fourier Series; Using the identity eqution with the Fouries Series, we can get the Phase Series.


The Fouries Series demonstrate the wave side of a function because its coordinaters are random. Giving an axies and its coordinator, if the coordinator are not constant, then the function is moving or "alive".


What's about the Phase Series? The Phase Series is different. Even though the function is moving, the sum of all the coordinators belong to its phase are stayed the same.
